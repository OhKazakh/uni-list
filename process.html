Research Universities and Programs:

Firstly, I would recommend to make a list of universities that interest you, like I did, considering factors like program offerings, location, size, and cost. Look at their official websites to understand their application requirements and deadlines.

Check Course Requirements:

Each program might have specific high school courses required for admission. Ensure you have a strong academic record that fulfills these needs.

Standardized Testing:

Many universities require standardized tests like the SAT or ACT for domestic students and IELTS or TOEFL for international students to assess English language proficiency. If required, register and prepare for these exams well in advance.

Start Building Your Application Profile:

Maintain strong grades throughout high school to keep your GPA competitive. Get involved in extracurricular activities, clubs, sports, or volunteer work that showcase your well-roundedness and passions.

Consider Scholarships & Financial Aid:

Look into scholarships and financial aid programs offered by universities, government agencies, or private organizations. Start researching and applying early to meet deadlines.
